The Neuroscience of Coding

Medulla Oblongata

Controls your heartbeat and breathing when you have to finish coding your website before the deadline

The base of the brainstem; controls heartbeat and breathing

Reticular Formation

Coding takes a long time and requires you to stay at the same place, staring at your computer screen, so reticular formation is necessary to remain focused in order to complete the task

A nerve network in the brainstem that plays an important role in controlling arousal

control arousal

Hippocampus

Being able to retain and recall memory regarding how to code (the specific rules or syntax of the coding language being used)

A neural center located in the limbic system that helps process explicit memories for storage

Angular Gyrus

Needed in order to attain the ability to read the code that is present

Involved in reading; receives visual information from the visual area and recodes it in auditory form, which Wernicke's area uses to derive its meaning.

Frontal Lobe

Sometimes you might come across a challenge, and so the frontal lobe is needed to make certain, appropriate judgments to reach a solution

The portion of the cerebral cortex lying just behind the forehead; involved in speaking and muscle movements and in making plans and judgments

Occipital Lobe

It is necessary to visually view the code to be able to see the progress, what's missing, and if any errors have been made

The portion of the cerebral cortex lying at the back of the head; includes the visual areas, which receive visual information from the opposite visual field

Amygdala

A little anxiety can develop when errors are present in your code, but you can't figure out how to fix them... It can cause you to get slightly aggressive after you spend half an hour, only to find out you just forgot a 'greater than' sign!

Two lima bean-sized neural clusters that are components of the limbic system and are linked to emotion

Corpus Callosum

Connecting the two hemispheres together, the corpus callosum allows the transfer of motor, sensory, and cognitive information to occur.

The large band of neural fibers connecting the two brain hemispheres and carrying messages between them

Cerebral Cortex

Some tasks are difficult to complete and they require thinking and may involve the algorithm process. The cerebral cortex is pivotal to have for processes such as thinking, perceiving, processing, and language comprehension which all play important roles in coding.

The intricate fabric of interconnected neural cells that covers the cerebral hemispheres; the body's ultimate control and information-processing center

Cerebellum

Coding requires A LOT of typing and the cerebellum can help with that. It also helps with coordinating eye movements and posture.

The "little brain" at the rear of the brainstem; its functions include processing sensory input and coordinating movement output and balance
